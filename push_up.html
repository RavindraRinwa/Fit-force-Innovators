PUSH UP Exercise

Steps

    1.Contract your abs and tighten your core by pulling your belly button toward your spine.

    2.Inhale as you slowly bend your elbows and lower yourself to the floor, until your elbows are at a 90-degree angle.

    3.Exhale while contracting your chest muscles and pushing back up through your hands, returning to the start position.

Befits

    1.The upper body muscles that come into play in the push-up are the deltoids of the shoulders, the pectoral muscles of the chest, the triceps and biceps of the upper arm, and the gluteal or hip muscles, and the erector spinae of the back.

    2.In daily life, you often need to push against objects, from doors to shopping carts. The functional fitness you develop with push-ups provides the strength needed to perform these movements.

    3.The abdominal muscles used to hold the body rigid during the push-up are the rectus abdominis and the internal and external obliques.
